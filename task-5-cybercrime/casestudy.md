# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A typical scenario involves a student receiving an email that appears to be from their bank or a well-known company. The email claims that there is an urgent issue with their account and asks them to click a link to verify their details. The link redirects to a fake website that looks identical to the original one. When the student enters their login credentials, the attacker captures the information.

Phishing attacks usually target students and working professionals who frequently use email and online banking services. Attackers often create a sense of urgency, such as warning about account suspension or suspicious activity, to pressure victims into acting quickly.

The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may lose money or have their personal data misused.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious online, verifying sources, and avoiding clicking on suspicious links.
